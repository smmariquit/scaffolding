My introduction to the Internet

into a computer. By ten, I was modding Minecraft. By twelve, tricking my friend with a fake Facebook website to
get his password and pull a prank.
Like a typical child who had very early unsupervised computer access, I really liked games. My parents were
really rooting for me to become a smart, successful person. They installed this educational game called

Minecraft
Minecraft is a sandbox game where you can create whatever you want and do whatever you like. As a kid, this
concept immediately appealed to me. I started out on Pocket Edition, hunched over a small smartphone
screen, but eventually, I made the jump to playing on my computer. That transition changed everything.
Remember how I learned how to program in Java? The thing is Minecraft was also programmed in Java. This
way, I knew how to make modifications to the game. This game became my avenue to create everything I
wanted to.
In Minecraft, there are these things called servers, kind of like virtual rooms people could host to let many
players play on the same world at the same time.
At the time, I was playing on a Dutch server called NLGVNL. Even though I didn't speak the language, I was
obsessed with how the server worked. I saw the "scaffolding" behind the curtain like the plugins, the
permissions, and how the server was administered.
One day, I was asked to contribute to a server's development. That was the moment the veil lifted. I realized a
server wasn't just a map. It was an intricate machine that needed a dutiful architect to take care over it..
This would eventually lead to me making my own server.
II. The Build: The making of HearthCraft
The Piso Net
In 2018, I decided to build my own world: HearthCraft. The timing was terrible. My laptop was broken, so I was
stuck coding Java on my phone and walking to the neighborhood "piso net" to push updates. I would sit in
